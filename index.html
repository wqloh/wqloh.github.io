Section 1: GDP & Life Expectancy

Life expectancy has a strong linear correlation with GDP per capita across all continents. Many African countries have low life expectancy and low GDP per capita, while many European countries have high life expectancy and high GDP per capita.

Section 2: Declining Populations

The populations of many advanced economies around the world are projected to decline in the coming decades leading up to 2100. They are projected to experience either decreasing rates of population increase or even population decrease altogether.

Section 3: Olympics Rep

The ratio of the number of athletes who participated in the 2024 Paris Olympics to the population of each country varies greatly across countries. Countries like Pakistan and Myanmar have disproportionately few athletes for their large population sizes relative to the rest of the world.

Section 4: VAT across Europe

A common gripe of consumers in the UK, but compared with the rest of Europe, a VAT of 20% turns out to be pretty standard. Interesting outliers include Hungary at 27% and Switzerland at 8.1%.
